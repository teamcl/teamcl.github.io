Cafeteria's Menu

Graphic and Service Design

The cafeteria at Srishti Institute of Art, Design and Technology was the setting for this project. It involved researching problem areas in the space and developing solutions for one. This project spanned two weeks.

Research

We created a miniature model of the cafeteria to understand the spatial layout. This was followed by a mapping of the activities that occur in the space. We used service design tools to document everything that happens in the cafeteria, but focused mainly on the front end of the system.

The college cafeteria gets extremely crowded during rush hours - the people crowding are fighting to be heard, and several of them do not know what they want to order, or what is available, until they reach the counter to place their orders. The existing menu in the cafeteria is a simple whiteboard that lists a few items with their prices. It is located at the counter, and people reading it only add to the crowd already present.

The final intervention was a redesigned menu.

Design Process

We created an illustrated menu board mounted on a wall for the school's cafeteria, to replace the existing small white board that was being used. The new menu covers all the items served along with the timings they are available at. Illustrations and colours were used to code items in the menu.

Final Product

Our final output was a menu. The new menu was away from the counter which ensured that the cafeteria was less crowded during lunch hours, it covered every item that the cafeteria provided, it was much larger so that it could be seen from half way across the cafeteria space and added to the asethetic appeal of a dining space.
